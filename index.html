ABOUT

Revived and Replinished

DownHearted Soul | Die Hard - Live Life contains different literary pieces that reflect the author's real life. A girl who wrote her life story in the art of literary pieces. All the struggles and pain, loneliness and tears. Songs became her best friend, rythm, harmony and melody became her comforter, lyrics became her voice.

Paper and pen are the one who listen to her cry, the one accepts her views, the only things that could patiently hold on to her wherever she goes, whenever she wish to pour down all the things she keep in her heart.

My Literary Pieces



The Race

Looking behind you, there's no one left
But looking forward, you know it's a worthwhile thing
Running as fast as you can, straight, focused,
trying to reach the end

A lot of things can use you stumble
On the way, you may become tired,
may become exhausted
As time pass by, you dropped a lot of water to the ground
But your eyes are looking straight forward to the highest point,
the finish line

Some of the runners already gave up
Walking back to where they came from
Some are stumbled, some are disturbed
Some are complaining, looking on the way and saying,
“It's still too far”
Without looking on how far they ran the race.

Some became weak and stopped running
Some also tried to go on, trying to reach their destination
Gratefully, you're still strong, full of energy and concentration
Your mind was set to the price if you survived and finish the race,
Your hope did not perish, you have a strong spirit.

Life is like a race, it's never been easy
You need a lot of endurance to survive and go on
There are many challenges that can cause you stumble
But if you fell down, you must learn to stand and go on.

~Pamela Tamayo Montilla



GRASS

Notice the grass that covered the ground
Down to the Earth, they preserve beauty
Small as they are, they show humility
Not as tall as the trees, but strong enough to endure a hurricane.

A strong wind strike them
Big flood cove them
Big creatures step on them
But still, they slowly rise up every after the rain

They might be covered with thick mud
When flood seasons arrive
But they humble themselves
Bowing down and wait till the sun will rise.

Why not low yourselves as grasses do?
Why not try to humble and accept the due?
Why not endure the pain and do not loss hope?
Why not bow down to let the typhoon pass?

If you want to be like a proud and strong tree
And exalt yourself high above those little grasses
If you proudly face the strong hurricane believing you can beat it
Will you be able to see the new things the next morning?

Uprooted, like the big tree, you will also be
Down to the Earth dead will be your end
Follow those little grasses on the ground
Humble yourself and you will survive.

~Pamela Tamayo Montilla



The Last Teardrop

With all the things happened from the past
I survived
I stood up firmly
I never tried to hide

With all those difficulties,
Someone lend me a hand
I found a relief,
It's in God's hand

I cried in vain, asking the same questions, WHY?
I'm depressed, I'm weak and so much tired
Feeling all the sorrows, I pity myself with that

Looking back, I realized
Those things are just challenges to continue the journey,
Difficulties in Life that will bring you lessons and courage to move on.

The last teardrop fell to the ground
This time I made a promise, this would be the last
This last teardrop is a new beginning
To recover, regain my strength, conquer my fear

This last teardrop tought me a lesson
Remembering that I'm not alone
God is wih me, He will never leave me
With this last teardrop, I am free.

~Pamela Tamayo Montilla



Wild Rose

Blood colored petals
Thorny stems and fragrance
Hidden deep in the forest
This wild flower of beauty

First look, it seems so gloomy
But staring to it, you'll see its real beauty
It's fragrance that filled the air around
Gives delight and pleasure to the heart

Those tiny thorns around her
Those small leaf blades on her stems
Those bloody petals that are clearly seen
A dream of fantasy in her hidden smile
was her charm

Among all those flowers and grasses
This little wild rose was so different
A simple beauty, purity is its wealth
Unrecognized but full of grace.

~Pamela Tamayo Montilla
